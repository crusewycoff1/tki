About Tau Kappa Iota

A brotherhood forged in tradition, dedicated to excellence, and committed to lasting bonds.

Tau Kappa Iota was founded on February 29th, 2024, born from the genuine desire of three individuals who recognized something extraordinary in their Friday night traditions. What began as three friends who simply enjoyed going above and beyond on Friday evenings evolved into a brotherhood that would redefine what it means to celebrate life, tradition, and camaraderie.

The origins of our fraternity trace back to a fateful evening when one of our founders donned a Hawaiian shirt for a night out. What started as a spontaneous fashion choice gradually transformed into the cornerstone of our identity. Week after week, the tradition grew stronger, the Hawaiian shirt becoming a symbol of our commitment to living life with enthusiasm and authenticity. These Friday nights, spent supporting local establishments and building unbreakable bonds, became known as Hawaiian Fridays‚Äîthe heart of TKI tradition.

On one particularly memorable evening, the three founders‚ÄîM. Woodson, Q. Hinrichs, and C. Wycoff‚Äîmade the decision to formalize what had become something greater than themselves. They drafted the Constitution of Tau Kappa Iota, a document that would enshrine the values, traditions, and vision of the fraternity. This constitution outlined sacred traditions including the Pilgrimage from the Bar, the creation of the TKI Mask, and the composition of an entrance essay by prospective members. It also established safeguards to protect the integrity of the organization from those who might seek to undermine its mission.

From those humble beginnings, Tau Kappa Iota has grown into a respected organization with chapters at Louisiana State University and Texas A&M University. We have remained true to our founding principles while expanding our reach, proving that authentic brotherhood built on genuine tradition can flourish and inspire others. Today, TKI stands as a testament to the power of friendship, the importance of tradition, and the enduring value of a brotherhood that celebrates life without reservation.

As we continue to grow, we remain committed to the vision of our founders: a fraternity where academic excellence meets social tradition, where brotherhood is earned and cherished, and where every Friday night is an opportunity to honor the values that define us.

Our Core Values

The pillars upon which Tau Kappa Iota stands

Brotherhood

We forge lifelong bonds built on trust, loyalty, and mutual respect. Our brothers support each other through all of life's challenges and triumphs, creating a network that extends far beyond our college years.

Academic Excellence

We are committed to scholastic achievement and intellectual growth. TKI members strive for excellence in the classroom, understanding that education is the foundation of future success.

Tradition

We honor and preserve the cherished traditions that define us, from Hawaiian Fridays to our sacred rituals. These traditions connect us to our founding and to each generation of brothers that follows.

Celebration

We embrace life's moments with enthusiasm and commitment. Every Friday night, we drink to the foam, celebrating brotherhood and supporting the local establishments that have become part of our tradition.

The Triumvirate

Tau Kappa Iota is governed by a Triumvirate of equal authority, each founder holding equal power and responsibility in guiding our fraternity.

M. Woodson

Founding Member of the Triumvirate

A visionary leader who helped establish the traditions and values that define TKI. Co-author of the TKI Constitution and guardian of our founding principles.

Q. Hinrichs

Founding Member of the Triumvirate

A driving force behind TKI's growth and expansion. Instrumental in establishing our chapter system and maintaining the integrity of our organization and Co-author of the TKI Constitution.

C. Wycoff

Founding Member of the Triumvirate

A dedicated architect of TKI customs, traditions, and events. Co-author of the TKI Constitution and steward of our cherished customs and ceremonial practices.

Our Chapters

Tau Kappa Iota is proud to have active chapters at two prestigious universities

Alpha Chapter

Louisiana State University

The founding chapter of Tau Kappa Iota, established in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. Home to the original Hawaiian Friday tradition and the birthplace of TKI's cherished customs. Alpha Chapter continues to set the standard for brotherhood and tradition.

Beta Chapter

Texas A&M University

Established in College Station, Texas, Beta Chapter exemplifies the growth and reach of TKI values. Our Aggie brothers uphold the same commitment to brotherhood, excellence, and tradition that defines Tau Kappa Iota.

Join Tau Kappa Iota

Become part of something greater

Tau Kappa Iota seeks individuals who embody our values of brotherhood, academic excellence, and respect for tradition. If you believe you have what it takes to join our ranks and contribute meaningfully to our legacy, we invite you to express your interest.

The path to membership begins with submitting an interest form. Those who demonstrate genuine commitment and alignment with TKI values will be personally contacted by current members. Selected candidates will be invited to attend a Hawaiian Friday, where the initiation process will begin. The traditions, rituals, and ceremonies that follow are sacred to our brotherhood and known only to initiated members.

We do not take membership lightly. Those who earn the privilege of wearing our letters join a brotherhood that demands excellence, loyalty, and dedication to our cherished traditions.
